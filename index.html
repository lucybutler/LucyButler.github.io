About Me

Hello! I’m Lucy Butler, a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Psychology of Misinformation Lab at the Network Science Institute at Northeastern University.

My research focuses on understanding the social and cognitive factors that influence belief in misinformation, and on developing effective strategies to mitigate its impact. I am particularly interested in what drives reliance on health-related misinformation, as well as how to effectively communicate accurate information to promote greater reliance on evidence-based health information.
I’m currently based in Boston but originally from Albany, Western Australia. In my free time, I enjoy crocheting, cooking, and spending time at the beach. I’m also unreasonably passionate about coffee (and how bad Boston’s coffee is).
